Kodaikaanal


Kodaikanal is a serene hill station in Tamil Nadu, set 2,000 meters above sea level in the Palani Hills. It is famous for its granite cliffs, forested valleys, waterfalls, and the star-shaped Kodaikanal Lake. Kodaikanal is a serene hill station nestled at an altitude of over 7,000 feet (2,133 metres) in the Palani Hills of the Western Ghats in Tamil Nadu, India. Popularly known as the "Princess of Hill Stations," it is celebrated for its mist-covered valleys, rolling green hills, and tranquil lakes. Originally established as a summer retreat by British bureaucrats and American missionaries in 1845, it remains one of South India's most popular getaways.
